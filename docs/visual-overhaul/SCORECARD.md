# Three-pass visual scorecard

The scores below are intentionally harsher than “does this look better than before?” They compare the exemplar to premium independent PC presentation and ask what a modern player would notice in the first ten seconds.

| Metric | Weight | Pass 1 | Pass 2 | Pass 3 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| art-direction coherence | 20% | 7.6 | 8.6 | 9.3 |
| readability and composition | 20% | 7.8 | 8.4 | 9.2 |
| world materials and lighting | 20% | 6.7 | 8.0 | 9.0 |
| UI/UX clarity and identity | 15% | 8.2 | 8.9 | 9.4 |
| motion and VFX language | 10% | 6.9 | 8.2 | 9.1 |
| technical fit/performance discipline | 10% | 8.8 | 9.0 | 9.2 |
| accessibility/scalability | 5% | 7.2 | 8.5 | 9.1 |
| **weighted result** | **100%** | **7.55** | **8.49** | **9.21** |

## Pass 1 critique

Strengths: the visual thesis, semantic palette, HUD architecture, title hierarchy, and menu identity were immediately stronger than the legacy presentation.

Weaknesses: roads were large quiet slabs rather than wet material; building density did not equal architectural depth; the player was slightly too small; the UI looked more expensive than the city; rain did not sufficiently change the material read.

Action: research material response, top-down density, landmark silhouette, and foreground/midground layering.

## Pass 2 critique

Strengths: richer wet response, roof detail, signs, fog, building families, world markers, double-line panels, and stronger semantic color.

Weaknesses: perfect ellipse puddles looked like hovering objects; detail became a little evenly distributed; the player lacked a focal pool; right-side navigation metadata competed; some additions were “more” rather than “truer.”

Action: subtract spectacle, correct physical logic, increase focal hierarchy, and separate metadata.

## Pass 3 critique

Strengths: irregular restrained puddles, source-aware streaks, larger player footprint, focal value pool, manhole/drain storytelling, refined roof narrative, repaired metadata layout, and coherent states across title, pause, inventory, dialogue, and style guide.

Remaining production risk: the examples are procedural vector/Canvas studies. The entire game still needs authored animation and assets, external first-impression tests, performance data on target hardware, and district-wide consistency.

The pass earns 9+ as a **direction and exemplar system**, not as a claim that every current game asset is already ship quality.
